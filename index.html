    Lecture 6 - Renal pharmacology
    Drug class Example(s) Mechanism of action (MOA) Indications Key adverse effects / precautions
    ACE inhibitors
  • Captopril
  • Enalapril
    		• Block angiotensin‑converting enzyme → ↓ Ang II formation → ↓ preload (blood volume) and ↓ afterload (peripheral resistance). Also ↑ bradykinin (enhances vasodilation). First‑line for hypertension in patients with heart disease, diabetes, or chronic kidney disease.
    • Dry cough
    • Angioedema
    • Hyperkalemia (↓ aldosterone)
    • Orthostatic hypotension
    • Altered Taste
    • contraindicated in pregnancy.
    Angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs)
  • Losartan
  • Valsartan
    		• Competitive antagonists at AT1 receptor → block Ang II effects (vasodilation, block aldosterone). Do not increase bradykinin. First‑line option for hypertension (esp. diabetes, HF, CKD).
    • Hypotension
    • Hyperkalemia
    • Less cough/angioedema than ACEi
    • contraindicated in pregnancy.
    • Avoid combining with ACEi.
    Renin inhibitor Aliskiren (Tekturna®) Selective renin inhibitor → blocks conversion of angiotensinogen to Ang I. Hypertension.
    • May cause angioedema (less than ACEi)
    • contraindicated in pregnancy, do not combine with ACEi/ARB
    • metabolized by CYP3A4.
    Other CVS agents (brief)

  • Aspirin: cardioprotection.
    • Aspirin: affects hemostasis (dose/interaction dependent).
    • Colchicine: GI and drug interactions (details not expanded here).
