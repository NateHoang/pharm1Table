Lecture 3: Local Anesthetics
Drug Class Onset Duration Metabolism / Notes Max dose (per appointment)
Lidocaine Amide 3–5 min Moderate (prolonged with epinephrine)
  • Metabolized in liver by microsomal CYP‑450
  • Active metabolites M.E.G.X. and G.X.
  • Vasodilator (use with epinephrine)
    		•  500 mg
    Mepivacaine Amide 3–5 min Short (~20 min without vasoconstrictor)
  • Metabolized in liver by CYP‑450 (like lidocaine)
  • Slight intrinsic vasoconstriction — may be used without epinephrine
    		• 400 mg
    Prilocaine Amide 3–5 min 10 min (no vasoconstrictor) → up to ~90 min (with VC)
  • Metabolized in liver and kidneys via amidases
  • Produces ortho‑toluidine (risk of methemoglobinemia, esp. G6PD)
    		• 600 mg
    Articaine (Septocaine®) Amide (contains ester group) 1–6 min
  • Moderate
  • Rapid clearance (t½ ≈ 20 min)
    		•
  • Metabolized mainly in blood by plasma carboxyesterase and to lesser extent in liver (CYP) — suitable in renal dysfunction
  • Contains both ester & amide moieties
    		•  No absolute max (limited by epinephrine)
    Bupivacaine (Marcaine®) Amide 2–10 min Long (≈ 90–180 min)
  • Metabolized in liver by amidases to inactive metabolites
  • High vasodilator activity (often paired with epinephrine)
    		•  90 mg
    Benzocaine Ester (topical) Topical, rapid —
  • Ester metabolized by plasma pseudocholinesterase
  • Low systemic absorption in topical gels (20% benzocaine examples)
    		• — [topical product doses vary]
    Procaine (Novocain®) Ester 6–10 min 15–60 min
  • Ester (pseudocholinesterase metabolism)
  • Significant vasodilation
  • Highly allergenic (PABA)
    		•  —
    Chloroprocaine (Nesacaine®) Ester 5–15 min 30–90 min
  • Rapidly metabolized by plasma esterases
  • Used epidurally (e.g., obstetrics)
    		•  —
    Tetracaine Ester Short onset Longer duration (high potency) Ester class (plasma metabolism, PABA risk) — often used in spinal anesthesia —
    Epinephrine (vasoconstrictor) Adrenergic agonist N/A
  • Prolongs LA duration
  • Hemostatic agent at high conc.
    		•
  • Acts on α and β receptors
  • Stimulates CV system
  • Overdose → CNS/cardiac stimulation
  • Interacts with TCAs and nonselective β‑blockers
  • Max total epinephrine healthy = 0.2 mg, severe CVD = 0.04 mg
    		• Dose limit: 0.2 mg (healthy) / 0.04 mg (ASA III–IV)
    Levonordefrin Synthetic vasoconstrictor N/A Prolongs LA (used mainly with mepivacaine)
  • Mainly α2‑agonist
  • Less cardiac/CNS stimulation than epi
  • Combined with mepivacaine 1:20,000
    		•  —
    Phentolamine Mesylate (OraVerse) Alpha‑adrenergic antagonist (LA reversal) N/A Reverses soft‑tissue anesthesia (shortens duration)
  • Dispensed 0.4 mg / 1.7 mL cartridge
  • Given 1:1 ratio (reversal : used LA)
  • Side effects — injection pain, CVS effects, headache
    		•  Use as indicated (product dosing)
